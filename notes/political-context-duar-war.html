Political Context of the Duar War

The Anglo-Bhutan War of 1864–1865 emerged from a long period of political tension along the frontier between Bhutan and the plains of Bengal. These tensions developed through disputes over authority, taxation, and control of the Duars.

Bhutanese Control of the Duars

For centuries Bhutan exercised varying degrees of authority over several Duars along the Himalayan foothills. These frontier tracts functioned as gateways connecting the Bhutan hills with the plains of Bengal and Assam.

British Expansion Towards the Himalayan Frontier

During the nineteenth century the British East India Company expanded its administration into northern Bengal. As British authority approached the foothills, interactions with Bhutanese frontier authorities increased.

Escalation of Frontier Disputes

Frequent disputes over taxation, raids, and jurisdiction created tensions between British officials and Bhutanese authorities. These unresolved issues ultimately led to the outbreak of the Anglo-Bhutan War in 1864.
