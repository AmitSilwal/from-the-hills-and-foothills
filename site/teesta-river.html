Teesta River

Overview

The Teesta River is one of the major Himalayan rivers of eastern India and forms the western boundary of the Dooars region.

Source

The river originates from glaciers in the eastern Himalayas in the Sikkim region.

Course

The Teesta flows through:

  • Sikkim
  • Darjeeling hills
  • northern West Bengal plains

It eventually enters Bangladesh and joins the Brahmaputra river system.

Dooars Regions Influenced

Within the Dooars region, the Teesta mainly influences:

  • western Dooars
  • Jalpaiguri region
  • areas near the foothills of the eastern Himalayas

The river system has influenced settlement patterns and agricultural development in these areas.

Geographic Importance

The Teesta historically forms the western boundary of the Dooars region, separating the Dooars from the Terai and the Sikkim–Darjeeling Himalayan zone.

Environmental Characteristics

  • major Himalayan river system
  • strong monsoon discharge
  • formation of fertile floodplains

The river also supports forest ecosystems and agricultural activity in the surrounding region.

Historical Importance

The Teesta valley played an important role in:

  • defining regional geographic boundaries
  • shaping settlement and agriculture
  • influencing early transportation routes.
