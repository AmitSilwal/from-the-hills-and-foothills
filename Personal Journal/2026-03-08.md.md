## Global Event Reflection – Iran–US Conflict

The recent tensions between Iran and the United States have drawn global attention. Conflicts in the Middle East rarely remain regional issues; they often influence the global economy, energy markets, and geopolitical alliances.

One of the most important aspects of such conflicts is their connection to global oil supply. A significant portion of the world’s oil passes through the Strait of Hormuz, which is geographically close to Iran. Any escalation or instability in this region can disrupt energy supply chains and push oil prices higher.

Rising oil prices can create ripple effects across the world economy. Countries that depend heavily on imported energy, including India, may experience higher fuel costs, transportation expenses, and inflationary pressures. In this way, even a conflict thousands of kilometers away can affect everyday life in distant countries.

Observing such events reminds me how interconnected the modern world has become. Regional conflicts are not isolated anymore; they influence global markets, political alliances, and economic stability.

This situation also highlights how geopolitics and energy security are deeply linked. Control over resources, strategic waterways, and political influence often play a major role in shaping international conflicts.

## Social Observation – Village WhatsApp Group

Today in my village WhatsApp group someone shared photographs of people distributing calendars featuring Mamata Banerjee, Abhishek Banerjee, and the local MLA of the block.

My first reaction was somewhat critical. I initially thought that distributing calendars is not a major achievement and does not represent real development. It seemed like a symbolic political activity rather than something that directly improves people's lives.

However, after thinking for a while, another perspective came to my mind. Such activities actually reflect the strength of grassroots political organization. Mamata Banerjee appears to have built a strong network of local party workers across West Bengal. These workers remain active at the community level, maintaining visibility and connection with ordinary people.

This type of local engagement demonstrates how political leadership relies on a structured and loyal grassroots team. Even small actions like calendar distribution serve as tools for maintaining political presence and public recognition.

In that sense, these activities reveal the effectiveness of organized political mobilization at the village level.

## Human Observation

While looking at the photos more closely, my attention gradually shifted away from the political activity and toward the people in the images themselves.

I started observing their faces, body language, and expressions. Many of them appeared simple and innocent. Some looked physically older than their actual age. Their faces seemed to carry the marks of years of work, responsibility, and perhaps hardship.

Their bodies also reflected the passage of time. Some individuals looked tired or worn out, as if life had demanded a lot from them. This observation made me think about how different life circumstances shape people's physical and emotional appearance.

Village life often involves hard physical work, economic uncertainty, and limited access to healthcare or resources. Over time these realities can become visible on people's faces and bodies.

Looking at the photographs in this way turned the image into more than just a political scene. It became a glimpse into the everyday lives and struggles of ordinary people.

## Personal Reflection

At one moment, a thought crossed my mind: if my parents had not provided me with a good education, my life path might have been very different. I might also have been participating in similar village activities, standing in similar photographs, and living a very different kind of life.

This thought immediately made me feel grateful toward my parents. Their efforts and sacrifices created opportunities for me that changed the direction of my life.

At the same time, seeing those images also reminded me that I am physically far from them. My father is now in his late seventies and my mother in her late sixties. Seeing people from the village made me realize how quickly time passes and how important family connections are.

For a moment I felt a sense of missing them deeply. It made me think about how I can build a stable life while also maintaining a strong connection with my parents and my roots.

These thoughts also made me reflect on the balance between pursuing personal ambitions and staying close to one's family and community. Education and career opportunities often create distance from one's place of origin, but emotional connections remain strong.

## Observation About Photography

Another interesting thought came to my mind while observing the photographs themselves.

The person who captured the photos did not seem to have much understanding of photography. The framing was not carefully composed, and the lighting conditions were not considered. The images appeared to be taken quickly without attention to composition or storytelling.

This made me realize that photography is actually an important skill. A well-composed photograph can capture not only people but also the atmosphere, emotions, and context of a moment.

Learning photography could be valuable for documenting real life situations, especially in regions like the Dooars. Photographs of landscapes, tea gardens, villages, people, and everyday activities could become important cultural and historical records in the future.

For a project focused on documenting the history and culture of the Dooars region, photography could play an important role in preserving visual memories of places and communities.

---

## Reflection

Education provides opportunities and broader perspectives, but it also creates a certain distance from one's roots. The challenge in life may be to maintain a balance between personal growth and connection to one's origins.

Moments like today remind me that life is not only about progress and ambition but also about remembering where one comes from and appreciating the people who made that journey possible.
