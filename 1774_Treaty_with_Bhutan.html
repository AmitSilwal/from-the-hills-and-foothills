1774 – Treaty between the East India Company and Bhutan

Type

Event

Summary

The Treaty of 1774 was concluded between the East India Company and the Bhutanese authorities following the conflict that began with the Bhutanese invasion of Cooch_Behar in 1772.

After British forces expelled Bhutanese troops from Cooch Behar, the Tashi Lama of Tibet intervened diplomatically and requested the British Government to cease hostilities against Bhutan.

The mediation led to negotiations between the two sides, resulting in a peace agreement in April 1774.

Key Terms of the Agreement

The treaty established peace between the two powers and included the following arrangements:

  • Restoration of peaceful relations between Bhutan and the East India Company
  • Bhutanese withdrawal from Cooch Behar
  • Re-establishment of diplomatic contact between Bhutan and British India

The agreement also created an opportunity for the British to pursue commercial relations with Tibet through Bhutan.

Diplomatic Consequences

The favourable tone of communications from the Tashi Lama made a strong impression on the British administration in Bengal.

As a result, Governor-General Warren_Hastings decided to send a diplomatic mission to Tibet to explore the possibility of trade relations.

This led directly to the 1774_Bogle_Mission_to_Tibet.

Historical Significance

The Treaty of 1774 was significant because it:

  • ended the immediate conflict between Bhutan and the East India Company
  • established formal diplomatic communication between the two governments
  • opened the way for British diplomatic missions to Tibet
  • marked the beginning of British strategic interest in the Himalayan frontier.
