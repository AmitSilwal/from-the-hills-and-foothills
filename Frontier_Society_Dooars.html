Frontier Society in the Dooars

Before British annexation in 1865, the Dooars formed a frontier region controlled by Bhutan.

The region was administered through a system of frontier passes known as the Duars.

Bhutanese officials known as Jungpens controlled the passes and collected tribute from the plains.

Settlement remained limited due to:

  • dense forests
  • malaria
  • difficult terrain.
