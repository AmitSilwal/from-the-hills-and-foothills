1792 Bijni Dispute

Overview

The Bijni dispute was a frontier conflict involving the Bhutanese authorities and the state of Bijni in the late eighteenth century. The dispute reflected the unstable political conditions that existed along the Dooars frontier.

The conflict also attracted the attention of the British East India Company, which was increasingly involved in regional affairs.

Background

Bijni was a small frontier state located near the foothills of the eastern Himalayas.

Bhutan attempted to assert authority over certain territories associated with Bijni and the surrounding frontier areas.

These actions created disputes regarding tribute, jurisdiction, and territorial control.

Nature of the Conflict

The dispute involved:

  • Bhutanese attempts to control frontier territories
  • disagreements over tribute and taxation
  • conflicts between Bhutanese officials and local authorities

The instability of the frontier made the situation difficult to resolve.

British Interest

The British East India Company monitored these developments closely because they affected the stability of the frontier.

Conflicts such as the Bijni dispute contributed to British concerns about Bhutanese influence in the plains.

Historical Significance

The Bijni dispute illustrates the fragile political balance that existed in the Dooars region before British annexation.

Repeated conflicts of this kind eventually contributed to growing tensions between Bhutan and British India.
