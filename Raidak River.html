Raidak River

Overview

The Raidak River is a major Himalayan river flowing through Bhutan and the eastern Dooars region of northern West Bengal.

Source

The river originates in the Himalayan ranges within Bhutan.

Course

The Raidak flows southward from Bhutan into the plains of the Dooars and eventually joins the Brahmaputra river system.

In parts of its course the river divides into multiple channels.

Dooars Regions Influenced

Within the Dooars, the Raidak flows through:

  • Alipurduar region
  • Kalchini region
  • eastern Dooars forest belt

These areas later developed into important plantation and forest zones.

Environmental Characteristics

  • Monsoon-driven flooding
  • shifting river channels
  • rich alluvial deposits

These features contribute to the ecological diversity of the eastern Dooars.

Historical Importance

The Raidak valley later became part of the expanding tea plantation belt during the colonial period.
