Labour Lines in Tea Estates

Overview

Labour lines were the residential settlements established within tea plantations to house the labour force working on the estates.

These settlements formed the basic social and spatial structure of plantation society in the Dooars.

Labour lines developed during the expansion of tea plantations in the late nineteenth century after the British annexation of the Dooars.

Related: Plantation Society Dooars Migration To Dooars Tea Plantations In Dooars

Structure of Labour Lines

Tea estate labour settlements were typically arranged in rows of small houses known as "lines".

Characteristics included:

  • rows of small workers' quarters
  • housing provided by plantation management
  • separate sections for different worker groups
  • proximity to tea fields

Each group of houses was often called a line.

Population Composition

The inhabitants of labour lines consisted mainly of migrant labourers brought from different parts of India and Nepal.

Major communities included:

  • Nepali
  • Adivasi groups from Chota Nagpur
  • tribal communities from central India

These communities later formed the core population of plantation settlements.

Social Organization

Life in the labour lines developed its own social structure.

Important features included:

  • strong community networks
  • shared cultural practices
  • plantation-based labour identity
  • dependence on estate management

Over time, labour lines became permanent settlements.

Cultural Life

Labour lines became centres of cultural interaction.

Different communities brought their own traditions, including:

  • festivals
  • music and dance
  • language and food traditions

This created the unique cultural diversity seen in the Dooars today.

Importance for Dooars History

The labour line system explains:

  • the settlement pattern of tea estates
  • the ethnic diversity of plantation regions
  • the development of modern Dooars communities.

Many modern towns and villages in the Dooars originated from plantation labour settlements.
