# Research Methodology

## About the Project

From the Hills and Foothills is an independent research and documentation initiative focused on the historical landscape of the Eastern Himalayan foothills, particularly the Dooars region and adjoining hill areas.

The project aims to collect, organize, and document historical references related to the region and gradually build a structured digital archive of sources, places, and historical events.

---

## Research Approach

The archive is built through systematic reading of historical sources and extraction of relevant information related to the region.

The focus is on identifying and documenting:

- historical events
- geographical locations
- administrative developments
- frontier interactions
- references to settlements and communities

Each reference is recorded with attention to its original source.

---

## Sources

The project primarily relies on historical materials such as:

- district gazetteers
- colonial administrative reports
- settlement records
- travel narratives
- historical publications
- archival documents related to the Himalayan frontier and the Dooars region

All sources are documented and referenced within the archive.

---

## Organization of the Archive

Research notes are organized into structured categories such as:

- historical events
- geographical locations
- timelines
- thematic notes
- source references

This structure helps gradually build a searchable and navigable archive of information related to the region.

---

## Project Status

This is an ongoing research and documentation project.

Materials and references are being added progressively as research continues.
