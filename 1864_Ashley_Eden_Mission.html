Ashley Eden Mission (1864)

Overview

The Ashley Eden Mission of 1864 was a diplomatic mission sent by the British Government of India to Bhutan in an attempt to resolve longstanding frontier disputes.

The failure of this mission became the immediate cause of the:

1864_Duar_War

Background

Relations between Bhutan and British India had deteriorated due to recurring frontier problems.

These included:

  • Bhutanese raids into the plains
  • disputes over tribute and taxation
  • conflicts involving frontier states such as Bijni
  • boundary disagreements in the Dooars region

The British Government hoped that a diplomatic mission might resolve these issues.

The Mission

Ashley Eden was appointed as the British envoy and sent to Bhutan to negotiate a settlement with the Bhutanese authorities.

However, the mission encountered hostility and difficult conditions during its negotiations.

According to British accounts, Eden was pressured into signing an unfavourable agreement.

Consequences

The British Government regarded the treatment of the mission as unacceptable.

The failure of the negotiations convinced British authorities that diplomacy had failed.

Shortly afterwards the British Government launched the military campaign known as the:

1864_Duar_War

Historical Significance

The Ashley Eden Mission represents the final diplomatic attempt to resolve frontier disputes between Bhutan and British India.

Its failure directly led to the outbreak of war and the subsequent annexation of the Dooars by the British.
