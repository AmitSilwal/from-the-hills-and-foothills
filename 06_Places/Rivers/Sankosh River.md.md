# Sankosh River

## 🟢 Quick Facts
- **Type:** Himalayan river  
- **Region:** Eastern Dooars boundary  
- **Flow Direction:** North (Bhutan hills) → South (plains)  
- **Role in Dooars:** Eastern boundary river  

---

## Description
A major river forming the eastern boundary of the Western Dooars.  
It originates in the Bhutan hills and flows southward, marking an important geographical and administrative division within the Dooars region.

---

## Course & Flow
1. Originates in the Bhutan hills  
2. Flows southward into the Dooars  
3. Forms boundary between Western and Eastern Dooars  
4. Continues into the plains  

---

## Importance
- Separates Western and Eastern Dooars  
- Acts as an important geographical boundary  
- Influences regional administration and settlement patterns  
- Supports agriculture and local livelihoods  

---

## Physical Features
- Seasonal variation in flow  
- Carries sediments from hills  
- Prone to flooding during monsoon
