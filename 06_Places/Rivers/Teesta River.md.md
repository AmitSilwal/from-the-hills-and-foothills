# Teesta River

## 🟢 Quick Facts
- **Origin:** Sikkim (Himalayan glaciers)
- **Flows through:** Sikkim → Kalimpong → Dooars → Bangladesh
- **Joins:** Brahmaputra (Jamuna)
- **Final flow:** Bay of Bengal (via Ganga system)
- **Role in Dooars:** Western boundary river

---

## Overview
A major Himalayan river forming the western boundary of the Dooars region.  
It connects the high Himalayas with the plains of Bengal and plays an important role in shaping the geography, settlement, and economy of the region.

---

## Course & Journey
1. Origin in Sikkim (glacier-fed)  
2. Flows through Kalimpong hills  
3. Enters Dooars region  
4. Moves into Bangladesh plains  
5. Joins Brahmaputra (Jamuna)  

---

## Why It Matters
- Defines boundary of [[Western Dooars]]  
- Influenced tea plantation expansion  
- Supports local communities and livelihoods  
- Causes seasonal floods  
- Influences settlement and agriculture  

---

## Physical Features
- Fast-flowing in upper course  
- Carries heavy silt and debris  
- Changes course frequently
