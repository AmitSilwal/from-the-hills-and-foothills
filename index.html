About the Project

From the Hills and Foothills is an independent documentation and research initiative focused on the historical landscape of the Eastern Himalayan foothills, particularly the Dooars region and adjoining hill areas.

The region has a complex past shaped by frontier interactions, migration, colonial administration, and the growth of tea plantations. Despite its historical importance, much of this history remains scattered across colonial records, gazetteers, and archival sources.

This project seeks to gradually collect, organize, and document references related to the history, geography, and cultural life of the region, and to build a structured digital archive that may be useful for researchers, students, and anyone interested in the history of the Himalayan foothills.

Research Focus

The project currently focuses on historical sources related to the frontier history of the Dooars and the broader Himalayan foothills.

  • Frontier relations between Bhutan and the British in the Dooars
  • Historical geography of the Himalayan foothills
  • Administrative history of the Dooars region
  • Migration and settlement in the foothill areas
  • Cultural histories of communities living in the region

As research progresses, the archive will also explore themes such as the expansion of tea plantations and the development of settlements across the Dooars.

Archive

The archive will gradually publish research notes, timelines, maps, references, and other materials related to the history of the Eastern Himalayan foothills.

At present, the project is in an early research phase. Historical sources are being read and notes are being organized to build a structured archive of references and events.

Materials will be added progressively as research develops.

Sources

Research materials for the project are drawn from historical publications and archival records such as district gazetteers, administrative reports, travel narratives, settlement records, and other historical documents related to the Himalayan frontier and the Dooars region.

Selected references and research notes are maintained in the project's repository.
