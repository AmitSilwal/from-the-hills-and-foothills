Jaldhaka River

Overview

The Jaldhaka River is an important Himalayan river flowing through Bhutan and the central Dooars region of northern West Bengal.

Source

The river originates in the eastern Himalayan region near the Sikkim–Bhutan border.

Course

The Jaldhaka flows through: - Bhutan - Kalimpong region - the central Dooars plains of northern West Bengal

It eventually joins the Brahmaputra river system through Bangladesh.

Dooars Regions Influenced

Within the Dooars, the Jaldhaka flows through areas including:

  • Malbazar region
  • Nagrakata region
  • parts of the Jalpaiguri Dooars

These areas later became important zones of tea plantation development.

Environmental Characteristics

  • Seasonal flooding during the monsoon
  • Formation of fertile alluvial soils
  • Supports forest ecosystems and wildlife habitats

Historical Importance

The Jaldhaka valley became an important zone for: - tea plantation expansion - settlement growth - forest resource use during the colonial period.
