# Ashley Eden Mission – Journey Phase (1863–64)

## Context

This phase marks the **initial execution of the mission**, from preparation at Darjeeling to arrival at Paro.

It is the first stage of:

→ [[Ashley_Eden_Mission_1863_64.md]]

---

## Preparation at Darjeeling

- Eden arrived in Darjeeling in November 1863  
- Organised transport and logistics  
- Mission team included:
  - Captain Goodwin Austen  
  - Captain Lance  
  - Dr. Simpson  
  - Cheeboo Lama (interpreter)  

- Escort of around 100 men  

---

## Lack of Bhutan Response

- No reply from Bhutan authorities before departure  
- No official reception or guide arranged  

👉 Early sign of diplomatic resistance  

---

## Entry into Bhutan

- Mission enters mountainous terrain  
- Initial reception uncertain  

---

## Difficulties Encountered

- Severe terrain and weather  
- Snow-covered passes  
- Coolies suffered exhaustion and death  
- Transport and supply challenges  

---

## Bhutanese Resistance

- Officials attempted to stop the mission  
- Movement delayed and controlled  
- Escort reduced  

---

## Loss of Control

- Mission became dependent on local authorities  
- British authority weakened progressively  

---

## Arrival at Paro

- Mission finally reaches Paro

## Historical Significance

- Shows failure beginning during journey itself  
- Demonstrates geographical and political challenges  
- Weakens British negotiating position before talks begin
