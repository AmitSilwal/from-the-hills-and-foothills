# Preparation for Ashley Eden Mission (1863)

## Context

By the early 1860s, relations between British India and Bhutan had significantly deteriorated due to prolonged frontier instability.

This included:

- repeated Bhutanese raids into British territories  
- abduction of inhabitants from frontier regions  
- disputes over tribute and control of the Dooars  
- failure of earlier diplomatic efforts  

These developments are part of:

→ [[Frontier_Conflicts_1828_1864.md]]

---

## Decision to Send a Mission

- In 1862, the Government of India decided to send a new diplomatic mission to Bhutan  
- The objective was to resolve frontier disputes peacefully  
- This decision followed repeated failures of earlier measures  

---

## Appointment of Ashley Eden

- Ashley Eden was appointed as envoy  
- Tasked with negotiating directly with Bhutan authorities  
- Expected to secure a stable agreement  

---

## Strategic Context

At this stage:

- British patience with frontier disturbances was declining  
- Frontier instability was becoming unacceptable  
- Military action was being considered as a possible alternative  

---

## Expectations

The British Government expected that:

- diplomacy would succeed where earlier efforts had failed  
- Bhutan would agree to terms ensuring frontier stability  

---

## Outcome

The mission was prepared and dispatched in 1863

→ [[Ashley_Eden_Mission_Journey_Phase_1863_64.md]]

## Historical Significance

- Represents the final diplomatic attempt before war  
- Shows British preference for negotiation before military action  
- Marks escalation of frontier tensions
