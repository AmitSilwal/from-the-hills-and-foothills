# Ashley Eden Mission – Paro to Punakha Phase (1864)

## Context

This phase represents the **second stage of the Ashley Eden Mission**, when the envoy reached Paro and proceeded towards Punakha.

It follows:

→ [[Ashley_Eden_Mission_1863_64.md]]

---

## Arrival at Paro

- Bhutan authorities summoned Cheeboo Lama  
- Mission treated with **insolence and disrespect**  

---

## Interaction with Bhutan Officials

- Eden met:
  - Penlow (Governor of Paro)  
  - Ex-Penlow  

- Interactions were tense and controlled  

---

## Conditions at Paro

- Description of:
  - Paro fort  
  - town and marketplace  
  - valley and monasteries  
  - proximity to Tibet  

👉 Indicates strategic importance  

---

## Signs of Conflict

- Dispute with a police official  
- Continued disrespect  
- No proper diplomatic protocol  

---

## Departure Toward Punakha

- Mission leaves Paro toward capital  

---

## Attempt to Stop the Mission

- Government messengers intercepted Eden  
- Tried to prevent further movement  

👉 Clear political resistance  

---

## Key Route Event

- Crossing of Dokiew Lah Pass  
- Noted for traditional significance  

---

## Approach to Punakha

- Mission proceeds despite resistance  
- Moves toward negotiation phase  

---

## Key Patterns

- Increasing hostility  
- Attempts to control mission  
- Lack of diplomatic respect  
- Delay tactics
