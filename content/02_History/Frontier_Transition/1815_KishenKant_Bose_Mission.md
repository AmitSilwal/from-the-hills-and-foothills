# 1815 Kishen Kant Bose Mission

## Overview

The mission of Kishen Kant Bose to Bhutan in 1815 was an attempt by the British Government to negotiate a settlement of frontier disputes between Bhutan and British India.

The mission reflected continuing efforts to stabilize relations along the Dooars frontier.

---

## Background

During the early nineteenth century the frontier between Bhutan and the British-controlled plains remained unstable.

Disputes continued over:

- raids into frontier territories
- tribute claims
- jurisdiction in the Dooars region

These problems led the British Government to pursue diplomatic negotiations.

---

## The Mission

Kishen Kant Bose was sent as an emissary to Bhutan with the aim of discussing frontier disputes and establishing improved relations between the two governments.

The mission sought to:

- address frontier tensions
- negotiate settlements regarding territorial disputes
- improve diplomatic communication between Bhutan and British India.

---

## Outcome

Although the mission represented a diplomatic effort to resolve conflicts, it did not produce a lasting settlement of frontier disputes.

Problems along the Dooars frontier continued during the following decades.

---

## Historical Significance

The mission illustrates the continued attempts by British authorities to resolve frontier disputes through diplomacy.
