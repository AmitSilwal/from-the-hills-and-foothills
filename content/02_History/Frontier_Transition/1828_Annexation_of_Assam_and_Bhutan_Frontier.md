# 1828 Annexation of Assam and Bhutan Frontier Relations

## Context
For several years after the mission of Kishen Kant Bose in 1815, there was little recorded communication between the British Government and Bhutan.

The situation changed after the First Burmese War when the British expelled the Burmese from Assam and took control of the region.

## What Happened
In **1828**, the British Government assumed control of **Lower Assam**.

By doing so, the British also inherited the political relations and disputes that previously existed between the Assamese rulers and neighbouring hill states, especially **Bhutan**.

## Frontier Situation
During the earlier period of political instability in Assam:

- Border tribes frequently descended from the hills
- They occupied land in the plains
- The Assamese rulers were unable to stop them

Among these tribes, the **Bhutanese benefited the most**, gaining control over several tracts of land in the plains known as the **Dooars**.

## Importance of the Dooars
The **Dooars** were fertile plains located at the foothills of the Himalayas.

Control of these territories became the main reason for the gradual involvement of the British Government with Bhutan.

## Historical Significance
The British annexation of Assam in **1828** fundamentally changed the political dynamics of the Bhutan frontier.

From this point onward, disputes over the Dooars and frontier raids increasingly strained relations between the British and Bhutan governments.

These tensions eventually contributed to the **Dooar War (1864–1865)**.
## Booree Goomah Duar Incident

One of the earliest frontier conflicts occurred soon after the British annexed Assam in **1828**.

A Bhutan official known as **Doompa Raja**, who controlled the **Booree Goomah Duar**, entered British territory and abducted several villagers. This incident was recorded as one of the first serious confrontations between Bhutan frontier officials and the British administration.

The event demonstrated that Bhutan frontier officers continued earlier practices of cross-border raids even after the political authority in Assam had changed.

### Significance

- One of the **first frontier conflicts after the British annexed Assam**  
- Showed the instability of the new British–Bhutan frontier  
- Contributed to growing tensions over the control of the Dooars
