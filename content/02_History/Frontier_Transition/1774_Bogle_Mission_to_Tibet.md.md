# 1774–1775 – Bogle Mission to Tibet

## Type
Event

## Summary

Following the conclusion of peace between the East India Company and Bhutan in **1774**, the British administration sought to establish diplomatic and commercial relations with Tibet.

The Governor-General of Bengal, **[[Warren_Hastings]]**, organized a diplomatic mission to Tibet. The mission was placed under the leadership of **[[George_Bogle]]**, a young civil servant of the East India Company.

The objective of the mission was to explore the possibility of establishing **commercial relations between Bengal and Tibet**, which the British believed could expand trade across the Himalayan frontier.

---

## Journey Through Bhutan

The mission departed from **Calcutta on 6 May 1774**.

Bogle travelled through:

- [[Cooch_Behar]]
- the Bhutan frontier
- Bhutanese administrative centres including **Tassisudon (Tashichho Dzong)**

He remained there for some time waiting for permission to proceed into Tibet.

Eventually the mission was allowed to continue northwards.

---

## Arrival in Tibet

Bogle reached the court of the **[[Tashi_Lama]]** (Teshoo Lama) at **Tashilhunpo Monastery** in Tibet in **October 1774**.

The Tashi Lama received the British mission in a friendly manner.

Bogle stayed in Tibet until **April 1775**, developing cordial relations with the Tibetan authorities.

---

## Objectives of the Mission

The mission sought to:

- establish trade relations between Bengal and Tibet
- improve diplomatic relations with Tibetan authorities
- explore commercial routes through Bhutan and the Himalayan frontier

The British hoped that trade with Tibet might include goods such as:

- wool
- musk
- gold dust
- silk
- horses
- Himalayan products

In return, British and Indian manufactured goods could be exported northwards.

---

## Outcome

Although the mission was diplomatically successful and friendly relations were established with the Tashi Lama, **no long-term commercial treaty was finalized**.

After the death of the Tashi Lama in **1780**, the political situation in Tibet changed, and sustained trade relations with Bengal did not develop.

However, the mission laid the foundation for future diplomatic contact, including the **[[1783_Turner_Mission_to_Tibet]]**.

---

## Historical Significance

The Bogle Mission was important because it represented:

- the first British diplomatic mission to Tibet
- an attempt to establish Himalayan trade routes
- an early stage of British strategic interest in the Bhutan–Tibet frontier.
