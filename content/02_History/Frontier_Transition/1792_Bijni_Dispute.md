# 1792 Bijni Dispute

## Overview

The Bijni dispute was a frontier conflict involving the Bhutanese authorities and the state of Bijni in the late eighteenth century. It reflected the unstable political conditions along the Dooars frontier.

The conflict also attracted the attention of the British East India Company, which was increasingly involved in regional affairs.

***

## Background

Bijni was a small frontier state located near the foothills of the eastern Himalayas.

Bhutan attempted to assert authority over territories associated with Bijni and surrounding frontier areas.

These actions created disputes regarding tribute, jurisdiction, and territorial control.

***

## Nature of the Conflict

The dispute involved:

- Bhutanese attempts to control frontier territories  
- Disagreements over tribute and taxation  
- Conflicts between Bhutanese officials and local authorities  

The instability of the frontier made the situation difficult to resolve.

***

## British Interest

The British East India Company monitored these developments closely because they affected frontier stability.

Conflicts such as the Bijni dispute contributed to British concerns about Bhutanese influence in the plains.

***

## Historical Significance

The Bijni dispute illustrates the fragile political balance in the Dooars region before British annexation.

Repeated conflicts of this kind contributed to growing tensions between Bhutan and British India.

These tensions ultimately formed part of the background to the:
