# Sankosh River

## Overview
The Sankosh River is one of the largest rivers flowing through the eastern Dooars and forms an important geographic boundary in the region.

## Source
The river originates in the Himalayan mountains within Bhutan.

## Course
The Sankosh flows southward from Bhutan into the plains of the Dooars and continues into Assam before joining the Brahmaputra river system.

## Dooars Regions Influenced
Within the Dooars, the Sankosh flows through:

- eastern Alipurduar region
- Kumargram area
- the Bhutan–Assam frontier zone

## Geographic Importance
The Sankosh River historically marks an important regional boundary.

It forms the **eastern limit of the Bengal Dooars and separates them from the Assam Dooars**.

## Environmental Characteristics
- strong seasonal flow during monsoon
- formation of extensive floodplains
- rich forest and wildlife habitats

## Historical Importance
The river historically functioned as:
- a frontier boundary
- a natural division between administrative regions of the Dooars.
