# Torsa River

## Overview
The Torsa River is one of the major Himalayan rivers flowing through Bhutan and the Dooars region of northern West Bengal. It is one of the most important rivers shaping the central Dooars landscape.

## Source
The river originates in the Himalayan ranges within Bhutan.

## Course
The Torsa flows southward from Bhutan into the plains of the Dooars and continues through northern West Bengal before eventually joining the Brahmaputra river system.

## Dooars Regions Influenced
Within the Dooars, the Torsa flows through areas including:

- Alipurduar region
- Cooch Behar region
- central Dooars plains

These regions later developed important tea plantations and settlements.

## Environmental Characteristics
- strong monsoon flow
- seasonal flooding
- formation of fertile alluvial floodplains

The river also supports forest ecosystems and wildlife habitats in the Dooars.

## Historical Importance
The Torsa valley became an important zone for:

- settlement expansion
- tea plantation development
- transport and trade routes in the colonial period.
