Dooars History Timeline (1772–1865)

This timeline presents the major historical events of the Dooars region, highlighting the evolving relationship between Bhutan and the British Empire. It covers key developments such as early diplomatic interactions, frontier conflicts, the annexation of Assam, Bhutanese raids, and the events leading to the Dooar War of 1864.

Understanding this timeline helps trace how the Duars region transitioned from Bhutanese influence to British control, culminating in the Treaty of Sinchula (1865), which reshaped the political landscape of North Bengal and the Eastern Himalayas.
