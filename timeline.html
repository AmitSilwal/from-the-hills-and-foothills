Historical Timeline of the Dooars

This timeline presents selected historical events related to the Dooars region and the Eastern Himalayan foothills. It will expand as research continues.

18th Century

1772 — Bhutan invasion of Cooch Behar
Bhutanese forces entered Cooch Behar, leading the ruler of Cooch Behar to seek assistance from the British East India Company.

1774 — Treaty between Bhutan and the East India Company
Following the conflict involving Cooch Behar, a treaty was concluded between Bhutan and the East India Company.

1783 — Bogle Mission
George Bogle was sent by the British to Tibet through Bhutan to explore diplomatic and trade relations.

Late 18th Century

1792 — Bijni Dispute
A dispute involving the Bijni region illustrated tensions in the frontier areas between Bhutan and neighbouring territories.

Early 19th Century

1815 — Mission of Kishen Kant Bose
Kishen Kant Bose was sent by the British authorities on a mission to Bhutan.

Mid 19th Century

1864 — Duar War
Conflict broke out between British India and Bhutan.

1865 — Treaty of Sinchula
The war concluded with the Treaty of Sinchula, through which Bhutan ceded the Bengal Duars to British India.
