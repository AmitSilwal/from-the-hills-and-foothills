# Dooars Forest Landscape

## Overview
Before the expansion of tea plantations and colonial settlement, the Dooars region was largely covered by dense forests. These forests formed part of the eastern Himalayan foothill ecosystem and supported rich biodiversity.

The forest landscape played an important role in shaping the geography, settlement patterns, and economic development of the region.

## Natural Environment
The forests of the Dooars developed under conditions of:

- high monsoon rainfall
- fertile alluvial soils
- numerous rivers descending from the Bhutan hills
- humid subtropical climate

These conditions created dense vegetation and diverse wildlife habitats.

---

## Major Forest Types
Historically, the forests of the Dooars consisted mainly of:

### Moist Deciduous Forests
- dominated by sal (Shorea robusta)
- mixed hardwood species
- dense undergrowth

### Riverine Forests
- located along riverbanks
- dominated by grasses, reeds, and flood-tolerant trees

### Foothill Forests
- found closer to the Bhutan hills
- more rugged terrain with thick vegetation

---

## Wildlife
The Dooars forests historically supported a wide range of wildlife, including:

- elephants
- rhinoceros
- tigers
- leopards
- deer species

Many areas later became part of protected forests and wildlife reserves.

---

## Human Interaction with Forests
Before large-scale colonial settlement, the forests were sparsely populated.

Local communities depended on the forests for:

- hunting
- forest produce
- shifting cultivation

The dense forests also limited large-scale agriculture.

---

## Colonial Transformation
After the British annexed the Dooars following the [[1865_Treaty_of_Sinchula]], large areas of forest were cleared for:

- tea plantations
- roads and settlements
- timber extraction

This transformed the Dooars from a forest frontier into a plantation landscape.

## Importance for Regional History
Understanding the forest landscape helps explain:

- why the Dooars remained sparsely settled before colonial rule
- how plantation agriculture expanded after forest clearing
- the ecological transformation of the region.
