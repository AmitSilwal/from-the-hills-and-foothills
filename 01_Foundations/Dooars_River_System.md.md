# Dooars River System

## Overview
The geography of the Dooars is strongly shaped by a network of Himalayan rivers descending from the Bhutan hills into the plains of North Bengal and Assam.

These rivers create the fertile alluvial plains that define the Dooars landscape.

The river system has historically influenced:
- settlement patterns
- forest distribution
- trade routes
- tea plantation expansion

## Major Rivers of the Dooars

The main rivers flowing through the Dooars region include:

- [[Teesta_River]]
- [[Torsa_River]]
- [[Jaldhaka_River]]
- [[Raidak_River]]
- [[Sankosh_River]]

These rivers originate in the **Himalayan and Bhutan hill systems** and flow southward into the plains.

---

## Geographic Structure of the Dooars

The Dooars region can be understood as a **series of river valleys between major Himalayan rivers**.

From west to east:

1. [[Teesta_River]]
2. [[Torsa_River]]
3. [[Jaldhaka_River]]
4. [[Raidak_River]]
5. [[Sankosh_River]]

These rivers divide the Dooars into multiple geographic zones.

---

## Environmental Characteristics

The river system produces:

- fertile **alluvial floodplains**
- seasonal flooding
- dense forest ecosystems
- rich wildlife habitats

Historically these conditions limited early settlement but later supported **tea plantation agriculture**.

---

## Historical Importance

The rivers played an important role in:

- defining **frontier boundaries**
- shaping **transport routes**
- influencing **location of early tea estates**

Many early settlements and plantations developed along river valleys.
