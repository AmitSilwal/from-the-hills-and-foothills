# Historical Timeline of the Dooars

This timeline presents selected historical events related to the Dooars region and the Eastern Himalayan foothills. The timeline will expand as research continues.

---

## 18th Century

**1772 — Bhutan invasion of Cooch Behar**

Bhutanese forces entered Cooch Behar, leading the ruler of Cooch Behar to seek assistance from the British East India Company.

**1774 — Treaty between Bhutan and the East India Company**

Following the conflict involving Cooch Behar, a treaty was concluded between Bhutan and the East India Company. This marked one of the earliest formal diplomatic interactions between the British and Bhutan.

**1783 — Bogle Mission**

George Bogle was sent by the British to Tibet through Bhutan to establish diplomatic relations and explore trade possibilities.

---

## Late 18th Century

**1792 — Bijni Dispute**

A dispute involving the Bijni region occurred during the late eighteenth century, illustrating tensions in the frontier areas between Bhutan and neighboring territories.

---

## Early 19th Century

**1815 — Mission of Kishen Kant Bose**

Kishen Kant Bose was sent by the British authorities on a mission to Bhutan, reflecting continued diplomatic engagement between the British administration and Bhutan.

---

## Mid 19th Century

**1864 — Duar War**

Conflict broke out between British India and Bhutan, commonly known as the Duar War.

**1865 — Treaty of Sinchula**

The war concluded with the Treaty of Sinchula. Bhutan ceded the Bengal Duars and certain hill territories to British India.

---

*This timeline is part of an ongoing research project and will be expanded as new references are documented.*
